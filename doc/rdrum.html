RDrum

Home Contents Synth Catalog Previous: MASA Next: Saw3

RDrum is an extension of the "Risset Drum", an additive technique developed by Jean Claude Risset for the synthesis of drum sounds. It is capable of producing realistic drum sounds, at least over a short range.

The original Risset Drum had 4 sine wave partials, one at the fundamental and the remaining three at specific non-harmonic frequencies. RDrum treats the fundamental separately from the other three partials and adds a noise component.

The RDrum components are:
  • Tone A, single sine/square wave
  • Tone B, 3 non-harmonic sine waves
  • Filtered noise

The controls for Tone A and Tone B are nearly identical. The Noise section adds a few additional parameters but it too is very similar to the other two sections.

Tone B

Tone B consist of 3 non-harmonic sine partials. The controls for Tone B are identical to those of Tone A with exception of the Tone control. For tone B the tone control sets the relative frequencies of the component partials. The "R" tick marks the classic Risset Drum frequencies.

Noise

The noise section consist of band-pass filtered white noise with the same envelope controls as Tones A and B.

RDrum Parameters

  • amp,   Linear output amp (0,2)
  • aRatio,   Tone A frequency ratio
  • aAttack,   Tone A attack time (0,2)
  • aDecay,   Tone A decay time (0,2)
  • aAmp,   Tone A amp (0,2)
  • aBend,   Tone A envelope bend (-1,+1)
  • aTone,   Tone A sine, square mix, 0=sine, 1=square
  • aVelocity,   Tone A velocity sensitivity
  • bRatio,   Tone B frequency ratio
  • bAttack,   Tone B attack time (0,2)
  • bDecay,   Tone B decay time (0,2)
  • bAmp,   Tone B amp (0,2)
  • bBend,   Tone B envelope bend amount (-1,+1)
  • bTune,   Tone B partial tuning (0,4)
  • bVelocity,   Tone B velocity sensitivity (0,1)
  • noiseRatio,   Noise filter track (0,16)
  • noiseBias,   Noise filter static value (0,20k)
  • noiseAttack,   Noise attack time (0,2)
  • noiseDecay,   Noise decay time (0,2)
  • noiseAmp,   Noise amp (0,2)
  • noiseRes,   Noise filter resonance (0,1)
  • noiseBend,   Noise envelope to filter (-1,+1)
  • noiseVelocity,   Noise velocity sensitivity (0,1)
