Saw3

Home Contents Synth Catalog Previous: RDrum Next: Sol

Saw3 is a straight forward subtractive synthesizer.

  • 3 Oscillators
  • Noise Source
  • Dual Filters
  • 2 ADSR envelopes
  • 2 LFOs

Oscillator Panel

Each of the 3 oscillators produces a different set of waveform, their controls however are nearly identical. For all three oscillators the Ratio tumbler sets the relative oscillator frequency.

OSC1

OSC1 generates a filtered sawtooth wave.
Wave,   filter cutoff, 0=filtered, 1=sawtooth
Amp,   OSC1 amplitude
Env1 -> Wave,   Envelope 1 modulation of waveform
LFO -> Wave,   LFO modulation of waveform
Env1 -> Amp,   Envelope 1 modulation of amplitude
The Inv,   check boxes inverts modulating signals

OSC2

OSC2 produces Pulse waves with pulse width modulations. The OSC2 controls are identical to those of OSC1.

OSC3

OSC3 generates a synced sawtooth signal. OSC3 controls are nearly identical to OSC1 and OSC2 with the following exceptions. OSC3 adds a bias tumbler to set a fixed frequency offset. The wave modulation signals are processed through a Lag processor.

OSC3 is not band limited, at high frequencies aliasing will occur.

Noise

The noise source is first filtered and then ring modulated with OSC3. This produces a somewhat rougher sound then the typical noise source.

Freq, filter cutoff
BW, filter band-width
Amp, noise amplitude
Env1->Amp, envelope 1 modulation of noise amplitude"

Pitch

Port, portamento time, (Not available in Poly1 keymode)
External, External control signal ->Pitch

Saw3 Filter Panel

The filter section contains two filters. The primary lowpass filter is a simulated Moog ladder filter. The secondary filter is a bandpass in parallel with the lowpass. The two filters share the same set of controls, for example moving the Res slider changes the resonance of both filters. The center frequency of the bandpass filter can be offset relative to the lowpass. If the lowpass has a cutoff of 4k, and the bandpass offset is 3, then the bandpass has a center frequency of 12k. Additionally there is a lag processor placed on the bandpass control inputs. Using lag imparts a time delay between changes in the lowpass and bandpass filters.

Freq,   fixed filter frequency
Env1->Freq,   Envelope 1 to filter frequency
LFO->Freq,   LFO to filter frequency
Xtern,   external control signal to filter frequency
Keytrack,   Filter key tracking
Offset,   Bandpass center-frequency offset from lowpass cutoff
Res,   filter resonance
Env1->Res,   Envelope 1 to filter resonance
LFO->Rex,   LFO to filter resonance
Mix,   Relative mix between low and band pass filters, 0=lowpass,1=bandpass
Env1->Mix,   Envelope 1 to filter mix
LFO->Mix,   LFO to filter mix

Inv checkboxes invert signal

Envelopes

There are 2 identical ADSR envelope generators. ENV1 is used to modulation, ENV2 is dedicated to the output envelope. See envelope time sliders

LFOs

There are two LFOs, one for modulation, the other dedicated to vibrato.
Freq LFO/Vibrato frequency
Delay LFO/Vibrato onset delay
Depth LFO amplitude/Vibrato depth
Sens Vibrato sensitivity
